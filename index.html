About eScholarship

eScholarship is UC's institutional repository and Open Access publishing platform.

eScholarship Publishing provides comprehensive publication services for UC-affiliated departments, research units, publishing programs, and individual scholars who seek to publish original, open access journals, books, conference proceedings, and other scholarship. The eScholarship Publishing program also supports two preprint servers, EarthArXiv and EcoEvoRxiv.

eScholarship Repository offers preservation and dissemination services for a wide range of scholarship including working papers, electronic theses and dissertations (ETDs), student capstone projects, and paper/seminar series.

eScholarship is managed by the California Digital Library (CDL) and is available without fees to UC-affiliated departments, research units, publishing programs, and scholars.

UC Open Access Policies

UC Open Access Policies cover scholarly articles and allow UC employees to retain rights in their work by granting the University a non-exclusive license to exercise rights under copyright, including distribution and display.

Publication Types Covered: The default covered types include journal articles, book chapters, and conference papers. Authors may deposit other materials they consider scholarly articles in their discipline.

Learn more about UC Open Access Policies

UC Publication Management System (UCPMS)

The UCPMS is used by UC authors systemwide to make it easier to identify open access policy articles and deposit them to eScholarship. The system emails authors and the delegated helpers biweekly if it has found new publications to claim and deposit to their department Open Access Policy Deposits series and UC Riverside Previously Published Works. CDL have worked with UC Riverside to ensure that the faculty and researchers who are likely to be publishing are receiving notifications.

UCR Author ID Cleanup Project

To reduce information overload for UCR authors, the UCR Library has implemented the Author ID Cleanup Project. Library students manually verify UCR author IDs—such as ORCID and Scopus Author ID—enabling auto-claim for verified IDs and reducing unnecessary reminder emails. The project is expected to finish in spring 2026.

Article-Level Metrics

eScholarship integrates with Altmetrics to show article-level engagement data including social media mentions, news coverage, and more.

ORCID iDs are displayed on individual article pages when available, helping connect works to author profiles.

eScholarship provides article views and downloads in its Metrics tab on each article page.

Authors receive quarterly statistics notifications for deposited publications, keeping them informed of their work's impact.
